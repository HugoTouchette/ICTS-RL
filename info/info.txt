Monte Carlo Methods

Topics to be covered:

- Sampling and statistical estimators
- Markov chain Monte Carlo
- Metropolis-Hastings algorithm
- Simulated annealing
- Stochastic gradient methods
- Applications: combinatorial optimization, likelihood estimation, thermodynamic integration.
- PyMC package

Textbooks/resources used:

- S. Asmussen, P. W. Glynn, Stochastic Simulation, Springer, 2010.
- J. S. Liu, Monte Carlo Strategies in Scientific Computing, Springer, 2001.
- D. J. C. Mackay, Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms, CUP, 2003.
- C. A. L. Bailer-Jones, Practical Bayesian Inference, CUP, 2017.
- D. Barber, Bayesian Reasoning and Machine Learning, CUP, 2012.

Prerequisites:

- Basic probability and statistics
- Basic programming skills in Python
- Markov chains (not necessary but recommended)

Expected background reading

- S. Ross, A First Course in Probability, Prentice-Hall, 2010.
